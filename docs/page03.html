Taj Mahal, Agra

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World, the Taj Mahal is an iconic symbol of love and architectural beauty.

Jaipur, Rajasthan

Known as the "Pink City," Jaipur is famous for its majestic forts and palaces, including the Amber Fort, City Palace, and Hawa Mahal.

Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

One of the oldest continually inhabited cities in the world, Varanasi is a spiritual hub for Hindu pilgrims and is renowned for its ghats along the Ganges River.

Kerala Backwaters

The backwaters of Kerala offer a serene and picturesque landscape of interconnected rivers, lakes, and canals, providing a unique and tranquil experience.

Goa

Famous for its pristine beaches, vibrant nightlife, and Portuguese-influenced architecture, Goa is a popular tourist destination for both domestic and international travelers.

Mumbai, Maharashtra

The bustling metropolis of Mumbai is home to iconic landmarks such as the Gateway of India, Marine Drive, and the Bollywood film industry.

Leh-Ladakh, Jammu and Kashmir

Known for its breathtaking landscapes, rugged terrain, and Buddhist monasteries, Leh-Ladakh offers adventurous activities like trekking and mountaineering.

Ranthambore National Park, Rajasthan

One of the largest national parks in India, Ranthambore is famous for its tiger population and offers wildlife safaris amidst ancient ruins and dense forests.

Khajuraho Temples, Madhya Pradesh

These UNESCO World Heritage Site temples are renowned for their intricate carvings and sculptures depicting various aspects of life and spirituality.

Mysore, Karnataka

Known for its opulent Mysore Palace, vibrant markets, and rich cultural heritage, Mysore is a must-visit destination for history and architecture enthusiasts.
